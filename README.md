### [12] A photodiode is a semiconductor device that converts light into electric current. It has two terminals, an anode and a cathode, that are connected to a p-type and n-type region of silicon, forming a p-n junction. When light falls on the photodiode, it generates electron-hole pairs in the depletion region of the p-n junction. These charge carriers are swept by the electric field and create a photocurrent that flows from the anode to the cathode. The photocurrent is proportional to the light intensity and the wavelength of the light. Photodiodes have various applications such as optical communication, optical sensing, solar cells, and photometry.
